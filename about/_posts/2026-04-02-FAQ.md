## About Me

<details>
<summary><strong>Who is Christophe VG?</strong></summary>
<p>
Christophe VG (Christophe Van Ginneken) is an Enterprise Architect from Belgium with over 25 years of experience in software architecture, technology consulting, and digital transformation. Based in Baal, Belgium, he helps organizations translate business strategy into valuable IT solutions.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What does an Enterprise Architect do?</strong></summary>
<p>
An Enterprise Architect ensures the realization of the client's dream according to best practices and good principles. This involves translating business vision into IT strategy, creating roadmaps that resolve conflicts and support stakeholders, and guiding transformations to deliver tangible business value.
</p>
<p>
The role spans the entire process: from capturing initial ideas and formalizing them into specifications, through engineering and construction phases, to the final implementation. Learn more in <a href="/about/I-Enterprise-Architect">I, Enterprise Architect</a>.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What's your professional background?</strong></summary>
<p>
I've been working with computers since 1983, starting with a Tandy Color Computer 2. My career spans:
</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Enterprise Architecture</strong> at organizations like Gosselin Group, Bank Van Breda, Acerta, and TUI</li>
<li><strong>Software Architecture</strong> for companies including UnifiedPost, SD Worx, and Telindus</li>
<li><strong>Embedded Systems &amp; IoT</strong> research at KU Leuven and University of Antwerpen</li>
<li><strong>Founder</strong> of 2Know BVBA (freelance architecture) and co-founder of Valid Solutions</li>
<li><strong>Creator</strong> of various hardware and software projects documented in <a href="/makes/">Makes</a></li>
</ul>
<p>
I hold a Master's in Computer Science from KU Leuven (2014), following a career transition in 2011 to pursue formal education in embedded systems.
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What makes your approach different?</strong></summary>
<p>
I describe myself as a <strong>problem seeker</strong> rather than just a problem solver. Before jumping to solutions, I dig deeper to uncover the true challenge. This prevents treating symptoms while underlying issues continue to cause harm.
</p>
<p>
I'm also <strong>technology agnostic</strong>—I don't believe in silver bullets or one-size-fits-all solutions. Every technology choice depends on the specific context, constraints, and goals. Read more in <a href="/about/Virtue-of-Technology-Agnosticism">The Virtue of Technology Agnosticism</a>.
</p>
</details>
