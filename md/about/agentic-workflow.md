# My Agentic Workflow

URL: https://christophe.vg/about/agentic-workflow/

This page summarizes how I work with AI agents. For the full story of my journey into agentic workflows, see [Hello Agents](/about/hello-agents/).

## The Short Version

I use AI agents as capable collaborators who implement my ideas faster than I can type them. They never sleep, know a lot about web development and programming, and learn through iteration. I review everything they produce. The final say is always human.

**What this means for you:** Unless explicitly noted, all content on this site is original. When AI assistance is involved, I'm transparent about it. Quality is guaranteed—I review everything before publication.

## My Approach

- **Iterate at least twice** — First attempts are never perfect
- **Give freedom, then review** — Let agents create, then critique their own work
- **Teach, don't micromanage** — Provide context, review results, explain corrections
- **Trust and verify** — Let agents explore solutions, verify outcomes

## Code of Conduct

When I work with AI agents, I follow these principles:

- **Human oversight** — All AI-generated content is reviewed by me before publication
- **Transparency** — Commits with AI assistance are attributed; AI-generated pages show the prompt
- **Original content stays human** — Recipes, sailing stories, photography—my voice stays mine
- **Quality assurance** — I catch errors and ensure accuracy

## Website-Specific Practices

On this website, AI agents help with maintenance tasks: fixing bugs, adding features, improving design, refactoring code, and occasionally creating entire pages. Here's how you can identify AI-assisted content:

**Commit Attribution**: Every commit involving a coding agent includes clear attribution in the commit message. Check the [git history](https://github.com/christophevg/christophe.vg/commits/master) to see what was done with AI assistance.

**Page Banner**: When an entire page was created by the coding agent, you'll see a banner at the bottom with the exact prompt I gave.
